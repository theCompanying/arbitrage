# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Executive Summary

This GTM strategy outlines our approach to launching an Amazon FBA arbitrage business sourcing from AliExpress and selling to Western markets. Focus: rapid product testing, data-driven selection, and lean operations.

---

## Target Customer

### Primary Buyer Persona

**Name**: Value-Conscious Prime Member

**Demographics**:
- Age: 25-45
- Income: $40K-$100K household
- Location: US, UK, Canada, Australia
- Behavior: Amazon Prime member, reads reviews, compares prices

**Psychographics**:
- Seeks convenience and fast shipping
- Willing to try new brands for good value
- Trusts Amazon's return policy
- Makes purchase decisions based on reviews and ratings

### Secondary Buyer Persona

**Name**: Niche Enthusiast

- Passionate about specific categories (fitness, pets, home organization)
- Willing to pay premium for specialized solutions
- Active in online communities and forums

---

## Product Strategy

### Phase 1: Product Selection Criteria (Months 1-3)

**Target Categories**:
1. Home & Kitchen (organizers, gadgets, accessories)
2. Electronics Accessories (cables, mounts, cases)
3. Fitness & Outdoor (resistance bands, yoga accessories)
4. Pet Supplies (grooming tools, feeders, toys)
5. Beauty Tools (brushes, applicators, organizers)

**Selection Filters**:
- AliExpress cost: $3-$15 per unit
- Amazon sale price: $15-$50 (3x-4x markup)
- BSR (Best Seller Rank): <50,000 in category
- Reviews on competing products: <500 (opportunity for differentiation)
- Product weight: <2 lbs (lower FBA fees)
- No dominant brand presence (top 5 products have <4.0 avg rating)

### Phase 2: Validation & Iteration (Months 3-6)

- Launch 3-5 products simultaneously
- Track metrics: conversion rate, review velocity, return rate
- Kill underperformers (ROAS <2.0 after 30 days)
- Double down on winners (increase inventory, optimize listings)

### Phase 3: Private Label (Months 6-18)

- Identify top 2-3 performing products
- Work with manufacturers on custom branding
- File for trademarks and brand registry
- Develop product line extensions

## Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Model

| Cost Component | Target % of Sale Price |
|----------------|------------------------|
| Product Cost (AliExpress) | 15-25% |
| Shipping to FBA | 5-10% |
| Amazon FBA Fees | 25-30% |
| Advertising (PPC) | 10-15% |
| **Gross Margin** | **30-40%** |

### Competitive Pricing Tactics

1. **Penetration Pricing**: Launch 10-15% below top competitors to gain initial sales velocity
2. **Dynamic Repricing**: Use repricing tools to stay competitive within ±5% of buy box
3. **Bundle Pricing**: Create 2-3 product bundles for 20% premium over individual items
4. **Promotional Pricing**: Lightning deals and coupons during launch phase (first 30 days)

## Launch Plan

### Pre-Launch (Week 1-2)

**Product Research & Sourcing**:
- [ ] Identify 10-15 candidate products using Helium 10 / Jungle Scout
- [ ] Order samples from 3-5 suppliers per product
- [ ] Evaluate quality, packaging, shipping time
- [ ] Negotiate pricing and MOQ with selected suppliers

**Listing Preparation**:
- [ ] Professional product photography (white background + lifestyle shots)
- [ ] Write SEO-optimized titles, bullets, descriptions
- [ ] Create A+ content (if brand registered)
- [ ] Set up PPC campaigns (auto + manual)

**Inventory Planning**:
- [ ] Order initial inventory (100-200 units per SKU)
- [ ] Ship to Amazon FBA warehouse
- [ ] Ensure inventory arrives 1 week before launch

### Launch Phase (Week 3-8)

**Week 3-4: Soft Launch**
- Activate PPC campaigns (auto-targeting, $20-30/day budget)
- Monitor impressions, clicks, conversion rate
- Adjust bids and keywords based on performance
- Goal: 5-10 organic sales/day

**Week 5-6: Review Generation**
- Enroll in Amazon Vine (if brand registered)
- Follow up with buyers via Buyer-Seller messaging
- Goal: 10-20 verified reviews per product

**Week 7-8: Optimization**
- Pause underperforming keywords/products
- Increase budget on winning keywords
- Optimize listings based on search term reports
- Goal: ACOS <30%, TACOS <15%

### Growth Phase (Month 3-6)

- Scale inventory for top performers
- Expand to additional product categories
- Launch in UK/Canada marketplaces
- Test external traffic (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok)

## Marketing & Promotion

### Amazon Advertising

**Campaign Structure**:
1. **Auto Campaigns**: Discovery, broad match (all products)
2. **Manual Exact**: High-intent keywords (top 20 per product)
3. **Manual Phrase**: Mid-funnel keywords
4. **Product Targeting**: Competitor ASINs with lower ratings

**Budget Allocation**:
- Month 1: $30-50/day per product (aggressive launch)
- Month 2-3: $20-30/day per product (optimization phase)
- Month 4+: Scale based on ROAS (target: 3.0+)

### External Traffic (Phase 2+)

- Facebook/Instagram ads for lifestyle products
- TikTok organic content (unboxing, product demos)
- Influencer micro-campaigns (1K-10K followers, product exchange)
- Pinterest for home/decor categories

### Promotional Tactics

- Launch discount: 20-30% off for first 50 customers
- Coupons: $2-5 off (ongoing)
- Lightning Deals: Monthly during growth phase
- Giveaways via social media (build email list)

## Risk Mitigation

### Key Risks

1. **Supplier Risk**: Quality issues, shipping delays, price increases
   - Mitigation: Multiple suppliers per product, order samples, maintain buffer inventory

2. **Amazon Policy Changes**: Fee increases, category restrictions
   - Mitigation: Diversify across categories, build DTC channel

3. **Competition**: Copycats, price wars, review manipulation
   - Mitigation: Build brand, differentiate with quality/packaging, focus on customer service

4. **Cash Flow**: Inventory ties up capital, Amazon 14-day disbursement
   - Mitigation: Maintain 3-month operating capital, negotiate supplier terms

5. **Product Liability**: Defective products, customer injuries
   - Mitigation: Product liability insurance, quality control inspections
